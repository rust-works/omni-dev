The gap that motivated this

~3.5 pp on the table vs the current C++-free path.

Cheap alternatives (whisper-base.en; sherpa-rs via C++) are either unproven or violate ADR-0033's C++-freeness gate.

This spike asks: is there a pure-Rust port of Parakeet that closes the gap?

What we measured — 3 probes

  • Probe 1 — Op coverage. Enumerate every op in conformer.py, attention.py, rnnt.py, parakeet.py; map each to candle.
  • Probe 2 — Weight conversion. MLX safetensors → candle-loadable safetensors. Throwaway converter; the mapping table is the artefact.
  • Probe 3 — Numerical parity. Encoder block-0 FFN1 sub-path: layer-by-layer MAE/max-AE between the fork and a candle stub.

Output: a go/no-go decision. Not the port. Encoder forward-pass parity is enough; TDT decoder + joiner are downstream.

The catch — CPU RTF — measured, resolved

Synthetic 24-block Conformer encoder, candle 0.10 stock gemm, Apple M1 Max, f32.

ThreadingT=750 (1 min audio)T=3750 (5 min audio)
Single-threadRTF 0.223RTF 0.273
Multi-thread (10 cores)RTF 0.081RTF 0.072

5.5× margin below the original abort gate of RTF 1.5 — even single-thread on the long-sequence (where attention's O(T²) kicks in).

Why the pessimistic estimate was wrong: candle's gemm on M1 Max sustains ~50 GFLOPs/s single-thread, not the 10 GFLOPs/s the back-of-envelope assumed. And #826's whisper-tiny.en RTF 1.3 was dominated by LocalAgreement-2 sliding-window re-inference cadence, not per-pass matmul — parakeet's streaming-native TDT decoder doesn't pay that cost.

CPU RTF caveats

The bench is honest about what it skipped — all together < 15 % overhead on top of the measured numbers:

  • Rel-pos bias matmul (~10–15 % of attention cost)
  • Depthwise k=9 conv (~1 % of conv module)
  • Mel-spectrogram frontend (one-time per chunk; small)
  • TDT decoder + joiner (~13 G MAC for 5 min audio — trivial vs encoder's ~600 G MAC)

Projected production: RTF ≤ 0.33 single-thread / ≤ 0.09 multi-thread on the same hardware.

Hardware sensitivity: M1 Max is fast. On older Intel laptops, single-thread ~1.5–2× higher (RTF 0.4–0.55); on weak ARM SBCs ~4–5× higher (RTF 1.0–1.5). For omni-dev's developer-workstation target, M1 Max numbers are representative.

Day-1 milestone in the feature task: re-run this bench on the production target's lowest-spec CPU to confirm the margin holds.

Streaming-latency reality (from COMPARISON.md)

Sherpa is ~12× faster on streaming at the same cadence — even comparing against GPU-accelerated parakeet.

This looked like evidence the candle/CPU port was doomed — but the synthetic encoder bench on the next slide showed the bottlenecks don't transfer. MLX/GPU's pain is per-chunk dispatch (scales with chunk-rate); candle-on-CPU's pain is gemm throughput on long sequences (favours larger T).

Recommendation: GO

All four probes cleared:

  • ✅ Op coverage — zero blockers
  • ✅ Weight conversion — 697 tensors, mechanical permute, loads in candle
  • ✅ Numerical parity — ~1e-3 relative error, well inside ±2 % WER bar
  • ✅ CPU RTF — 0.27 single-thread / 0.07 multi-thread on M1 Max (was the previously-open risk)

Recommend a Parakeet candle port as a feature task.

Day-1 milestone is now a validation — re-run the bench on the production target's lowest-spec CPU — not an abort gate.
